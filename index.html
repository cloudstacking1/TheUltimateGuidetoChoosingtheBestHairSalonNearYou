FAQs

1. What is the difference between balayage highlights and traditional highlights?

Balayage highlights are painted freehand onto the hair, creating a soft, blended effect that grows out naturally. Traditional highlights typically use foil or caps to section hair and produce more uniform streaks. Many clients prefer balayage because it offers a more natural-looking highlights result and lower maintenance.

2. How often should I get my highlights redone?

It depends on the technique, your hair growth, and the type of highlights (blonde, face-framing, ombre, etc.). Generally, balayage can last 3–6 months before needing a refresh, while more structured foil highlights may need touch-ups every 8–12 weeks. Your stylist at Honeybliss Salon can recommend a schedule tailored to your hair type and color goals.

3. Can highlights damage my hair?

Any coloring service has the potential to cause damage if not done correctly or if hair is already compromised. However, when done by experienced stylists using quality products (like at Honeybliss Salon), the risk is minimized. Deep conditioning treatments, bond repair therapies, and gentle aftercare help preserve hair health and reduce damage from hair color highlights.
